Snapshot

  • Best seasons Spring (late March–April) for blossoms, autumn (October–November) for crisp hikes.
  • Transit essentials Suica/PASMO works on most trains; Shinkansen seat reservations help on busy weekends.
  • Cash vs. card Cards widely accepted, but keep a small ¥10,000 reserve for small eateries and rural buses.

Highlights you shouldn't skip

Blend neon nights, temple calm, and crisp mountain air.

Kyoto stillness

Early mornings in Arashiyama's bamboo grove, golden light at Kinkaku-ji, and a slow tea at a machiya cafe. Reserve ahead for a small-group tea ceremony.

  • Don't miss: Fushimi Inari at sunrise, Nishiki Market snacks.
  • Photo spot: Philosopher's Path in late March.

Tokyo contrasts

Addictive ramen alleys, design museums, and pocket parks between skyscrapers. Hop on the Yamanote Line loop to see multiple neighborhoods in one day.

  • Don't miss: TeamLab Borderless, Ameya-Yokocho snacks.
  • Photo spot: Shibuya Sky at blue hour—book in advance.

Hokkaido outdoors

Wide-open trails, onsen culture, and dairy-rich cuisine. Base yourself in Sapporo and day trip to the dramatic cliffs of Shakotan Peninsula.

  • Don't miss: Niseko summer hikes, Sapporo beer garden.
  • Photo spot: Blue Pond near Biei in autumn fog.

Quick cultural cues

Small gestures that make a big impression.

  • Stations are quiet zones. Keep phone calls minimal and stand to the left on most escalators (right in Osaka).
  • Cash at shrines. Keep a coin pouch for offerings; bow once before entering torii gates.
  • Onsen basics. Wash thoroughly before entering the bath, and keep towels out of the water.
  • Restaurant rhythm. Water and tea are complimentary; pay at the register unless seated check is provided.
  • Trains run on time. Aim to arrive 5–10 minutes early for bullet trains; queue where floor markers show.
  • Trash is rare. Carry a small bag for recyclables; convenience stores are reliable for bins.

Food trail

Mix comfort staples with regional favorites.

Tokyo

  • Shoyu ramen: Try a counter in Ginza or Kanda.
  • Depachika picnics: Stock up at Tokyo Station's food halls.
  • Craft coffee: Kiyosumi-Shirakawa's roasters are a short train away.

Kyoto

  • Obanzai lunch: Seasonal side dishes, often under ¥1,200.
  • Matcha everything: From soft serve to layered parfaits.
  • Tofu hotpot: Yudofu by Nanzen-ji after a garden stroll.

Osaka

  • Takoyaki crawl: Dotombori for the classics; Tenma for locals.
  • Okonomiyaki: Hiroshima-style spots often offer counter seating.
  • Kushikatsu: No double dipping—use the cabbage leaves.

Hokkaido

  • Soup curry: Sapporo specialty with root vegetables.
  • Seafood don: Try dawn markets in Hakodate or Sapporo's Nijo Market.
  • Soft serve: Milk-rich cones at farm stands on rural drives.

7-day outline

Light structure you can tweak on the fly.

Day 1
Tokyo arrival. Check into a hub near Tokyo Station or Shinjuku. Evening izakaya in Omoide Yokocho and a quick peek at Shibuya Crossing.
Day 2
Tokyo design day. Spend the morning at Nezu Museum, lunch in Aoyama, and sunset views from Roppongi Hills or Shibuya Sky.
Day 3
Hakone or Kamakura escape. Hot springs with Fuji views or seaside temples. Return to Tokyo for dinner.
Day 4
Kyoto transfer. Ride the Shinkansen, drop bags, and explore Gion's lantern-lit alleys at dusk.
Day 5
Temples & tea. Morning at Fushimi Inari, mid-day at Nishiki Market, and a sunset stroll through Higashiyama.
Day 6
Arashiyama calm. Rent a bike for bamboo grove and river views, then soak at an onsen in Kurama or Kameoka.
Day 7
Osaka finale. Transfer to Osaka for flights, squeeze in a takoyaki crawl, and stock up on snacks at Kuromon Market.

Practical essentials

Keep these in your back pocket.

Transit toolkit

IC cards: Grab a Suica or PASMO before leaving the airport; works on metro, buses, and most vending machines.
Data: eSIMs are reliable—look for plans with 10–20GB for a week.
Luggage: Use takkyubin forwarding between cities to travel light.

Weather planning

Rain-ready: Pack a compact umbrella; convenience stores sell sturdy clear ones for ~¥500.
Heat tactics: In summer, carry a small towel and sunscreen—hydration drinks like Pocari Sweat are everywhere.
Layering: Evenings cool quickly in spring and autumn, so keep a light jacket in your daypack.

Etiquette refresh

Cash handoff: Use the small tray at registers; avoid passing bills directly.
Quiet corners: Lower voices on trains and in shared lodgings.
Thank you: "Arigatou gozaimasu" goes a long way—pair it with a light bow.
